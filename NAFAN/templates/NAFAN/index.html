When a user comes to the site they are either researchers, contributors, or someone who has yet to determine their role. This landing page provides for all three.

The New to NAFAN? link takes the user to a page that orientates them whether they are a researcher or a contributor. It is essentially a beefed-up About page that can also serve as the hub of an online support architecture. Button vs. hyperlink? Have a look at this: Button or Link

The Login is initially for contributors and site administrators. It has yet to be determined if researchers will be provided a login. It may be deemed useful for benefits such as saving search queries or maintaining a set of preferred repositories.

The results of the search need to be determined. I'm sure the discussion will be vigorous.

Footers

Footers are generally pointless. They take up real estate and on a site like this would not even be encountered often as the search results will likely be lengthy. The copyright is pointless as well. The individual finding aids may require their own copyrights, but web pages don't need them. Again, have a look at Google's landing page - no footer and no copyright.
